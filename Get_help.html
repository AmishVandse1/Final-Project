Need Help?

Step 1: Acknowledge the Problem

Acknowledging that there is a problem is often the most difficult step for gambling addicts to reach, especially if they’ve been deep in gambling addiction for years or decades. It’s also common for problem gamblers to deny their addiction and resist any suggestion to get help. There’s no perfect solution for overcoming this hurdle, as the addict must be honest with themselves and be ready to receive help. However, that doesn’t mean giving up; loved ones should still continue pointing out negative consequences and offering help when possible.

Step 2: Seek Professional Help

Professional help for gambling addiction varies based on severity and individual needs. Options include support groups, counseling with specialists, and, in severe cases, rehabilitation programs. Delaying help may exacerbate emotional, physical, and financial consequences.

Step 3: Get Outside Support

Outside support, from friends, family, coworkers, or mentors, plays a crucial role in gambling addiction recovery, providing motivation, accountability, and a non-judgmental listening ear.

Step 4: Follow Through With Treatment

Completing gambling addiction treatment is crucial for long-term recovery, as addicts might prematurely discontinue treatment when they start feeling better. Seeing treatment through ensures full benefits and equips individuals with vital relapse prevention skills, enhancing their chances of sustained recovery from problem gambling.

Step 5: Have an Aftercare Plan

After completing treatment for gambling addiction, returning to previous toxic friendships and risky situations increases the risk of relapse. Aftercare plans aim to support individuals in maintaining their recovery by reinforcing the lessons learned in treatment.
